  17. Internet Protocol version 6 (IPv6) is a new update from the version 4 protocol. This protocol allows packets to be send and recieved between two different areas(nodes) of the network. First of all, the new IPv6 version allows the creation of a lot more unique IP addresses than the previous version. The number of bits reserved for the addresses goes from 32 in IPv4 to 128 in IPv6. This would help the Internet of Things (IoT) markets as well as to allow even more people and devices access to the internet. Furthermore, IPv6 is also more secure than its predecessor in the way that it promotes the establishing of encryption and stops/prevents packet spoofing. IPv6 also handles packages faster and more efficiently than IPv4 which could free the way for a more streamlined connection and less cluttering.
